## 1. Direction

- **Retire "Offroad Parks" as the public name** — it was always a working codename.
- **Aesthetic: vintage / heritage badge** — national-park-poster / WPA, crest & roundel, topo + compass motifs, earthy palette. (Drives the merch system — see [BRANDING-merch-plan.md](BRANDING-merch-plan.md).)

## 2. The constraint landscape (what to avoid)

From the market + trademark landscape research:

- **Weak/crowded roots — don't build the name on these:** Trail/Trails/Trailhead, Offroad/Off-Road, Overland/Overlander, 4x4/4WD/ORV/OHV/UTV, "Planet/Nation/Bound" suffixes. Findable but unprotectable.
- **Legal landmines — automaker/tire marks (real infringement risk), avoid entirely:** Rubicon, Trailhawk, "Overland" (Jeep trim), Trail Rated, Baja, Raptor, Bronco, Wrangler, Power Wagon, TRD, Sasquatch. (Jeep/Stellantis is famously litigious.)
- **Incumbents to not echo:** onX Offroad, Trails Offroad, Gaia GPS, AllTrails, iOverlander, Trailforks, Overland Bound, FunTreks, RiderPlanet.
- **`.com` scarcity is the binding constraint.** Most short coined words are already registered. An open exact-match `.com` is rare and highly valuable — it materially narrows the field.
- **Must render as a badge:** legible one-color at ~1.5″ die-cut. A hard filter, since stickers/patches are the growth engine.
- **Opportunity/white space:** a *well-designed, discipline-neutral* brand (UTV/ATV/moto/4x4 together) around **destination/"places to ride"** — the OHV directories own data + SEO but have no brand.

*(Full landscape brief with sources archived in the Sprint 8 research; key sources listed in §6.)*

## 5. Recommendation — two strategic paths

The founder explored both ends of the spectrum. The choice is a genuine strategy fork:

**Ownable brand ◄──────────────────────► Pure SEO keyword**
`Rutwild` · `OHVAlmanac` · `OHVCompass` · `OHVAtlas` · `OHVAreas`

### Path A — Distinctive / ownable (stronger trademark, build brand equity)
**Lead: `Rutwild`** — coined, open `.com`, no trademark/business/handle conflict found, reads offroad ("rut"+"wild"), stamps cleanly as a one-color heritage badge. Backup `Rovadore`. **Pro:** a mark you can actually own and defend. **Con:** you build recognition from zero (no built-in search keyword).

### Path B — Descriptive / SEO (clearer + search-friendly, weaker trademark)
**Lead: `OHVAlmanac`** — the safest descriptive option: no same-space user found, "Almanac" is essentially unused in offroad (so it's the *most distinctive* of the weak descriptors), and it fits the vintage-heritage aesthetic (Old-Farmer's-Almanac register = a reference/guide to OHV areas). Open `.com`. Backup `OHVCompass` (viable; "compass" is on-theme + badge-native but generically crowded in software). **Avoid** `OHVAtlas` (Natural Atlas conflict) and `OffroadBeacon` (Beacon Offroad & Supply conflict). **Pro:** says what it is; helps comprehension/CTR. **Con:** descriptive marks are **weak and hard to enforce** — you'll share the lane with lookalikes, and keyword-in-domain is only a minor modern SEO factor.

### The honest tradeoff
Descriptive names win *early* clarity; distinctive names win *long-term* brand equity and legal defensibility (Gaia, onX, and Trailforks all coined or twisted a word rather than describing themselves). If SEO/clarity is the priority now, **OHVAlmanac** is the best-vetted descriptive pick. If a defensible brand matters more, **Rutwild** is the cleanest ownable pick.

**Decision (OP-104) is the founder's** — and should pass a gut check (say it at a trailhead; picture it on a tailgate patch). Whichever path: **verify handles manually + get attorney clearance before any spend.**

## 6. DECISION — `OHVCompass` *(chosen 2026-07-16, OP-104 — supersedes the earlier OHVAlmanac pick)*

**Selected name: `OHVCompass`** ("OHV Compass") — Path B (descriptive/discovery). Captures the founder's "scout / find your way / your next adventure" concept literally (a compass is the tool you explore with), is the **most badge-native** option (a compass rose is the core heritage-badge motif), and — unlike the evocative-descriptive names that kept hitting automaker landmines (Scout→Scout Motors, Recon→Honda Recon ATV, Atlas→Natural Atlas) — its only same-space vehicle collision is **Jeep Compass**, an *on-road* SUV, and "compass" is a diluted dictionary word. Accepted tradeoff: descriptive = a **weak, hard-to-enforce mark** (differentiation via brand + content, not legal exclusivity); expect a possible disclaimer or Supplemental Register outcome.

*(History: OHVAlmanac was picked first, then reconsidered on outside input; OHVScout and OHVRecon were dropped on trademark conflicts — see §3f/§3g. OHVCompass and OHVAlmanac are the same "weak descriptive" tier; Compass won on concept + badge fit.)*

- **Domain:** `ohvcompass.com` open — **and every checked TLD** (.co/.net/.org/.app/.io/.us) is open too.
- **Handles:** Instagram `@ohvcompass` and TikTok `@ohvcompass` both return "not found" (available). *(Still verify YouTube/X/Facebook/Reddit and re-confirm all at reservation time.)*

- **Descriptiveness/registrability** — "compass" is suggestive-to-descriptive for a *navigation* product; expect a possible disclaimer or **Supplemental Register** start; plan for acquired distinctiveness. This is the primary risk.
- **Jeep/FCA "Compass"** (Stellantis, Class 12 SUV) — assess likelihood-of-confusion for our app/SaaS/apparel; likely defensible given on-road goods + dilution, but confirm their class coverage (esp. any Class 9/25/42 filings).
- **Crowded "Compass" field** — Compass, Inc. (real estate, Reg. 6041375, Class 9 software), plus many software "Compass" marks; and **Van Compass** (offroad suspension) as an offroad-adjacent user. Clear FTO in 9/42/25.
