> ⚠️ **Legal caveat (read first).** Everything below is **preliminary orientation, not legal clearance.** Trademark findings come from secondary databases (Trademarkia/Justia/uspto.report) — the live USPTO system could not be queried directly — and social-handle checks were unauthenticated. "No conflict found" does **not** mean a name is free. **Before buying a domain, forming the LLC, or printing any merch, commission a formal trademark clearance search + opinion from a licensed attorney** covering Nice classes **9** (software), **42** (SaaS), **41** (online info/community), **25** (apparel), and **12/39** if we brand vehicles/trailers.

---

## 1. Direction (locked with founder)

- **Retire "Offroad Parks" as the public name** — it was always a working codename. We want a **fresh, coined, ownable** brand.
- **Aesthetic: vintage / heritage badge** — national-park-poster / WPA, crest & roundel, topo + compass motifs, earthy palette. (Drives the merch system — see [BRANDING-merch-plan.md](BRANDING-merch-plan.md).)

**Why coined, not descriptive:** the "Offroad / Trail / Overland / 4x4 + X" naming space is saturated *and* legally weak (descriptive marks are hard to register and impossible to police — you'd be one "Trail-something" among dozens). The defensible, badge-native path is a coined/arbitrary word (the **Gaia / onX** model).

---

## 2. The constraint landscape (what to avoid)

From the market + trademark landscape research:

- **Weak/crowded roots — don't build the name on these:** Trail/Trails/Trailhead, Offroad/Off-Road, Overland/Overlander, 4x4/4WD/ORV/OHV/UTV, "Planet/Nation/Bound" suffixes. Findable but unprotectable.
- **Legal landmines — automaker/tire marks (real infringement risk), avoid entirely:** Rubicon, Trailhawk, "Overland" (Jeep trim), Trail Rated, Baja, Raptor, Bronco, Wrangler, Power Wagon, TRD, Sasquatch. (Jeep/Stellantis is famously litigious.)
- **Incumbents to not echo:** onX Offroad, Trails Offroad, Gaia GPS, AllTrails, iOverlander, Trailforks, Overland Bound, FunTreks, RiderPlanet.
- **`.com` scarcity is the binding constraint.** Most short coined words are already registered. An open exact-match `.com` is rare and highly valuable — it materially narrows the field.
- **Must render as a badge:** legible one-color at ~1.5″ die-cut. A hard filter, since stickers/patches are the growth engine.
- **Opportunity/white space:** a *well-designed, discipline-neutral* brand (UTV/ATV/moto/4x4 together) around **destination/"places to ride"** — the OHV directories own data + SEO but have no brand.

*(Full landscape brief with sources archived in the Sprint 8 research; key sources listed in §6.)*

## 5. Recommendation

**Primary: `Rutwild`.** It's the only candidate that is coined/ownable **and** has an open exact-match `.com` **and** shows no trademark or business conflict **and** likely has an open handle — while also reading unmistakably offroad ("rut" + "wild"). It stamps cleanly into a one-color heritage badge. It was independently the #2 creative pick, so it doesn't sacrifice quality for availability.

**Backup: `Rovadore`.** Essentially tied on availability (open .com, coined, no conflict). Slightly longer/less punchy and lives near the busy "Rove/Rover" search cluster — a minor discoverability nick, not a blocker.

**Consider only with attorney sign-off: `Roverth`** (open .com, but the "Rover" resemblance sits *inside our own vehicle vertical* — a real likelihood-of-confusion + SEO risk) and **`Rovana`** (would require buying the aftermarket .com and accepting a taken handle).

**Decision note:** the *name* is the founder's call (OP-104) and should also pass a gut check — say it at a trailhead, picture it on a tailgate sticker. The data says **Rutwild** is the cleanest, most ownable path; if it feels right, it's the recommendation.
